1.1 Overview
In recent years, many students and expatriates in Egypt have faced challenges finding
suitable housing due to the difficulty of locating reliable brokers or available apartments
in new cities. Traditional methods often require physical visits, time-consuming searches,
and dealing with intermediaries who may not always be trustworthy. These obstacles
highlight the need for a digital platform that simplifies both the renting and buying
processes, providing users with a more accessible and transparent housing experience.
The proposed Broker Website serves as a comprehensive online solution that connects
property owners, brokers, and seekers—whether they are looking to rent or purchase
apartments—in an efficient and trustworthy way. Through a simple registration process,
users can create accounts and choose their roles as brokers who list their properties, or
seekers who wish to rent or buy. Once logged in, users can browse apartment listings that
include detailed information such as price, location, property features, and ownership
type (rent or sale), with advanced filtering options to refine search results.
The system supports both private and shared housing, allowing users to find standard
apartments for rent or sale, or shared units that cater especially to students and
individuals relocating from other cities. In the shared housing section, the platform
displays short profiles of current residents to help users identify compatible roommates
and make informed decisions before contacting the property owner or broker.
Additionally, the platform integrates an AI-powered assistant to enhance the overall
experience. By analyzing user inputs such as preferred location, price range, apartment
type, ownership preference (rent or buy), and the number of roommates, the AI refines
search results and suggests the most suitable options. This intelligent feature helps users
save time and effort, ensuring a faster, smarter, and more personalized experience when
renting or purchasing properties online.
1.2 Motivation
Finding suitable housing has become an increasingly complex challenge for individuals
in Egypt, especially students, workers relocating to new cities, and families searching for
secure and affordable options. Traditional rental processes still depend on brokers,
personal networks, or random online posts—methods that are often time-consuming,
unreliable, and lacking transparency. These outdated practices make property searching
stressful and inefficient, particularly as demand continues to rise across many cities.
The introduction of Egypt’s New Rental Law of 2025 has further emphasized the need
for modern digital housing solutions. With the new law restructuring old rental contracts,
redefining rent values, and setting clear timelines for contract termination, many tenants
and property owners have entered a period of transition. This shift has pushed a

To address these challenges, the Broker Website was developed as a centralized,

brokers, and property owners. It enables users to register, authenticate, and select their

seekers can browse, filter, and apply for suitable options. The platform supports both
private and shared housing, displaying essential property details such as price,
location, type, and owner information. Additionally, an AI-assisted recommendation
feature refines search results and suggests the most suitable rental or purchase
opportunities based on user preferences.
Technical Scope:
The project is designed as a web-based application built with modern technologies to
ensure scalability, performance, and data security. It utilizes a relational database to store
user and property information, supported by secure login, encryption, and verification
protocols to protect sensitive data. The system integrates AI models for intelligent

1.6 General constraints
Despite its potential benefits, the proposed system faces several constraints that must be
carefully considered during its design and implementation phases. These limitations may
influence the platform’s performance, accuracy, and scalability. The main constraints are
outlined below:
• Dependence on User-Generated Content:
The accuracy and reliability of property listings largely depend on the information
submitted by brokers, owners, and users. Inaccurate or misleading data could affect
trust and system credibility. Therefore, implementing strong verification and approval
mechanisms is essential to maintain content quality.
• AI Recommendation Limitations:
The effectiveness of the AI-assisted recommendation feature relies on the quality and
completeness of available data. Limited datasets or inconsistent user inputs may
reduce the precision and relevance of generated property suggestions.
• Infrastructure and Network Constraints:
The system’s performance and scalability depend on the quality of hosting
infrastructure and internet connectivity. In regions with unstable or limited network
coverage, user experience may be affected by slower response times or limited
accessibility.
• Data Security and Privacy:
As the platform handles sensitive personal and property-related information, ensuring
robust data protection is critical. Unauthorized access, data breaches, or misuse of
information must be prevented through secure authentication, encryption, and
database management practices.

2.1 Background
The real estate market in Egypt has witnessed remarkable growth over the past decade,
driven by rapid urban expansion, population increase, and government-led housing projects.
Despite this progress, the process of renting or finding apartments remains largely
traditional—relying on word of mouth, local agents, or physical visits. This manual approach
often leads to inefficiencies, limited accessibility for newcomers, and high dependency on
brokers who may not always offer transparent pricing or verified listings.
Digital Transformation in Real Estate :
In recent years, digital transformation has begun reshaping the real estate industry both
globally and locally. Online platforms have enabled users to browse available properties,
compare prices, and directly communicate with property owners or landlords. However, in
Egypt, many individuals—especially students, workers relocating from other cities, and
expatriates—still face major challenges when searching for accommodation remotely. The
lack of trustworthy platforms and effective verification systems often results in
misinformation, wasted time, and, in some cases, fraud.
• Growing smartphone and internet penetration has enabled broader access to online
property listings.
• Users increasingly expect digital services that emphasize convenience, transparency,
and trust.
• Traditional broker models are becoming less effective for modern renters who prefer
self-service solutions.
Technological Advancements and Smart Systems :
Technology—particularly Artificial Intelligence (AI)—is becoming a major driver in the
modernization of property management. AI can analyze user behavior, preferences, and data
patterns to provide personalized recommendations, improving the relevance and efficiency of
property searches. Additionally, automation tools can streamline critical processes such as
lead management, virtual tours, and tenant matching—features that most Egyptian platforms
have yet to adopt.
• AI and machine learning models enhance user experience through intelligent
matching.
• Automation tools reduce manual work for both property owners and administrators.
• Data analytics help identify market trends and user needs more efficiently.

3.1.1 Feasibility Study
The proposed Broker Website addresses a major housing challenge faced by students,
expatriates, and individuals moving across Egyptian cities. Traditional methods depend
on unverified brokers, physical visits, and inconsistent information, making the search
process slow and unreliable. By offering a centralized digital platform that displays
apartments for rent or sale along with shared housing options and short resident profiles
the system enhances transparency, reduces effort, and provides users with an organized
and efficient way to find suitable accommodation.
Economically, the project is highly feasible and affordable for a graduation-level system.
The estimated cost is relatively low, covering only essential expenses such as domain
registration, hosting, and basic deployment requirements, resulting in a total budget
between 1,400 and 4,000 EGP. Using free and widely available technologies, along with
relying on an external AI API rather than building a machine-learning model, minimizes
financial risk while still delivering strong functionality and real value to users.
From a technical and operational perspective, the system is straightforward to implement
using HTML, CSS, and JavaScript for the frontend, PHP for backend development, and
MySQL for data storage. This technology stack is stable, well-supported, and suitable for
features like account management, property listings, shared-unit details, and intelligent
search. Integrating an AI assistant through an external API keeps the platform
lightweight and easy to maintain, while regular backups and simple verification
processes ensure smooth daily operation. Overall, the platform is practical, manageable,
and capable of solving a real housing problem in the Egyptian market.
Estimated Cost (EGP)
The estimated cost of developing and deploying the Broker Website is low and suitable
for a student graduation project. The main expenses include domain registration (300–
450 EGP per year), web hosting on a shared server (900–2,400 EGP per year), and
optional AI API usage depending on the chosen provider (0–600 EGP). Additional minor
costs may include design tools or testing needs (200–400 EGP). Overall, the total
estimated cost ranges between 1,400 and 4,000 EGP, making the project economically
feasible and manageable for a student team.

4.1 Design and Implementation Constraints
4.1.1 Technical Constraints
• Server Performance and Load Handling:
As a web-based platform, all user interactions, property searches, and AI-based recommendations are
processed on the server side. The system must handle multiple concurrent users, especially during peak
browsing times. Limited hosting resources may affect response time and system availability.
• AI Recommendation Limitations:
The accuracy of the AI-powered property recommendation system depends on the quality and completeness
of user input data (location, budget, apartment type, and preferences). Incomplete or inaccurate data can
reduce the effectiveness of personalized recommendations.
• Internet Dependency:
The Broker Website requires a stable internet connection for browsing listings, uploading property images,
and receiving AI-based suggestions. Users in areas with poor connectivity may experience slower
performance or limited access.
• Browser and Device Compatibility:
The system must function correctly across major web browsers (Chrome, Firefox, Edge, Safari) and on
different devices (desktop, laptop, tablet, and mobile). User interface components, search filters, and listing
displays must be responsive and consistent.
• Database and Storage Constraints:
The platform relies on a relational database to store user accounts, property listings, images, and shared
housing profiles. Storage limits and database performance may restrict the number of listings and images that
can be handled efficiently.
37


===== PAGE 38 =====
4.1.2 Regulatory and Legal Constraints
• Data Privacy and Security:
The system stores sensitive user data such as personal information, contact details, and property ownership
records. It must comply with Egyptian data protection and privacy regulations, ensuring data encryption,
secure authentication, and restricted access.
• Real Estate Compliance:
All property listings must follow Egyptian real estate laws, including correct representation of ownership,
rental agreements, and pricing. False or misleading listings must be prevented through verification and
moderation mechanisms.
• Shared Housing and Cultural Regulations:
Shared housing listings must respect cultural and social norms, such as gender-based preferences and privacy
rules. User profiles in shared apartments must display only necessary information.
4.1.3 Resource Constraints
• Development Resources:
The system is developed by a small student team, which limits the speed of development and the complexity
of advanced features such as real-time messaging, payment systems, and automated verification.
• Budget Limitations:
The project is developed within a limited budget, which requires using low-cost domain services, shared web
hosting, and open-source development tools. The website, database, and property images are hosted on
affordable servers, which limits storage capacity, performance, and the number of users that can be supported.
External AI APIs are used instead of building complex in-house AI models to reduce overall development and
operational costs.
• Time Constraints:
The project must be completed within the academic semester, which limits the amount of testing, large-scale
deployment, and advanced feature development. Therefore, the system focuses on implementing the essential
functions such as user accounts, property listings, search and filtering, shared housing support, and AI-based
recommendations.

4.3 Risks and Risk Management
4.3.1 Technical Risks
• Incorrect or Poor AI Recommendations:
The AI-based recommendation system may generate inaccurate or irrelevant property suggestions due to
incomplete user data or limited historical information.
➤ Risk Treatment:
Improve input validation, refine recommendation rules, and allow users to manually search and filter
properties alongside AI suggestions.
• Server Downtime and Performance Issues:
The system may experience slow response times or downtime because of limited hosting resources or high
user traffic.
➤ Risk Treatment:
Use reliable hosting services, apply database optimization, and perform regular system monitoring and
backups.
• Browser and Device Compatibility Issues:
Users may face problems when accessing the platform through different browsers or mobile devices.
➤ Risk Treatment:
Apply responsive design and conduct cross-browser and device testing before deployment.
4.3.2 Security and Privacy Risks
• User Data Exposure:
Sensitive information such as user profiles, contact details, and property data may be at risk of unauthorized
access.
➤ Risk Treatment:
Use HTTPS, secure login mechanisms, encrypted storage, and access control policies.
• Fraudulent or Fake Listings:
Some users may submit misleading or fake property advertisements.
➤ Risk Treatment:
Implement user verification and listing approval processes before publishing properties.
40


===== PAGE 41 =====
4.3.3 Operational Risks
• Low User Trust and Adoption:
Users may hesitate to use the platform due to trust or usability concerns.
➤ Risk Treatment:
Introduce verified accounts, clear property information, and transparent communication features.
• Limited Technical and Financial Resources:
Budget and time constraints may delay development or restrict system improvements.
➤ Risk Treatment:
Use open-source tools, low-cost hosting, and phased development to focus on core features first.
4.3.4 Ethical and Market Risks
• Bias in Property Recommendations:
The AI system may prefer certain areas or price ranges due to unbalanced data.
➤ Risk Treatment:
Review recommendation logic regularly and ensure diversity in listing data.
• Over-Reliance on AI Suggestions:
Users may depend too much on AI recommendations and ignore manual searching.
➤ Risk Treatment:
Clearly label AI results as suggestions and provide full manual search and filtering options.
